Who Killed Laura Palmer?

A closer look at identity, mystery, and perception in Twin Peaks

In Twin Peaks, Laura Palmer is more than just a victim—she represents the hidden complexity beneath a seemingly perfect small town. Everyone claims to know her, yet each version of Laura is completely different.

As the investigation unfolds, it becomes clear that identity in Twin Peaks is fragmented. Laura exists as multiple narratives depending on who is remembering her, creating a layered and unsettling mystery.

Key Ideas

  • The illusion of perfection in small-town life
  • Multiple perspectives shaping one identity
  • The contrast between innocence and darkness

This ambiguity is what makes the show so compelling. Instead of giving clear answers, it forces the audience to sit with uncertainty and question what is real versus what is constructed.

Much like designing with Flexbox and Grid, understanding the full picture requires stepping back and seeing how all the pieces fit together.
